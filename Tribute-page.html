Dr Benjamin Nnamdi Azikiwe

Nigeria's First President

Brief info on Dr Zik

Nnamdi Benjamin Azikiwe,usually referred to as "Zik", was a Nigerian statesman and political leader who served as the first President of Nigeria from 1963 to 1966.Considered a driving force behind the nation's independence, he came to be known as the "father of Nigerian Nationalism"

Here is a timeline of Dr Nnamdi Azikiwe's life:

  • In 1904-Born in Kungeru,Northern Nigeria,born to both Igbo parents

  • In 1914-Nnamdi Azikiwe moved to Lagos where he attended Wesleyan Boys' High School

  • In 1920-He moved to Calabar with his dad where he began secondary school education at Hope Waddell Training college

  • In 1930-He graduated from Lincoln university with a BA in Political science after transferring from Howard university in 1929

  • In 1937-Azikiwe founded his own newspaper called "West African Pilot"

  • In 1943-The british council sponsored Azikiwe along with 7 other West African editors

  • In 1954-Azikiwe was selected as chief minister and became a premier of the Eastern region in 1954

  • In 1960-Azikiwe became the governor-general of Nigeria

  • In 1963-He became the first president of Nigeria

  • In 1972-Azikiwe became the chancellor of the university of Lagos

  • In 1978-He joined Nigerian People's Party(NPP)

  • In 1996-Azikiwe died due to long illness at the University of Nigeia Teachhing Hospital
